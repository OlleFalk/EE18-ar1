Gallery of the Moon

Pictures from exploration of the Moon.

Humanity's first steps beyond Earth were taken here. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin landed in the Moon's Sea of Tranquility on July 20, 1969 at 8:18 p.m. UTC. While astronaut Mike Collins orbited above, Armstrong and Aldrin set up experiments and collected 49 pounds (22 kilograms) of lunar rocks and soil – the first extraterrestrial samples returned to Earth

On Nov. 24, 1969, Apollo 12 astronauts Pete Conrad and Alan Bean demonstrated precision landing techniques with a successful touchdown on the Moon only 535 feet (163 meters) from the robotic Surveyor 3 probe. The astronauts brought parts of the spacecraft, on the Moon for more than two years.

Astronauts Alan Shepard (the first American in space) and Edgar D. Mitchell touched down on a lunar plain called Fra Mauro on Feb. 15, 1971. They collected 94 pounds (43 kilograms) of lunar soil and rock to be studied by more than 180 science teams in 14 nations back on Earth.

Four wheels on the Moon. Astronauts David Scott and James Irwin roamed the Hadley-Apennine region of the Moon in a Lunar Roving Vehicle, also known as a "Moon Buggy." They traveled more than 17 miles on the lunar surface and collected 170 pounds (77 kilograms) of lunar rock and soil.

Astronauts John Young and Charles Duke spent more than 71 hours exploring the Descartes region of the Moon in April 1972. Young and Duke collected 209 pounds (95 kilograms) of samples and drove the rover 16.6 miles (27 kilometers).

Gene Cernan and Harrison Schmidt explored the Moon for the final time of the Apollo program in December 1972. Cernan's final words on the Moon: "As we leave the Moon at Taurus- Littrow, we leave as we came and, God willing, as we shall return, with peace and hope for all mankind. Godspeed the crew of Apollo 17."
